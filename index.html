Track 1 agentic workflow for insurance claims

ClaimIQ turns raw claim emails into human-ready decision briefs.

A session-based agentic pipeline — Gmail inbox to adjuster-ready brief — with a mail guard, an OpenAI-backed router, and five specialist agents for intake, coverage, fraud, clinical triage, and adjuster assistance. Every stage writes an auditable event to BigQuery, stores evidence in Google Drive, and surfaces on a Looker Studio dashboard, while human approval stays mandatory on high-risk claims.

The problem

Claims teams are buried in unstructured emails, PDFs, policy wording, fraud risk, and clinical urgency.

ClaimIQ decomposes the workflow into specialist agents with deterministic fallbacks and guardrails. The demo playground below does not call the private prototype or real services. It mimics the solution behavior so reviewers can understand the agent flow immediately.

Insurance claim adjudication is slow because every claim combines customer language, attachments, medical evidence, legal policy clauses, fraud indicators, and routing decisions. ClaimIQ turns that messy front door into a structured, auditable, human-reviewable workflow across motor, health, property, travel, and life claims.

Solution approach

ClaimIQ acts like a coordinated expert claims team.

No single model is asked to be a policy lawyer, SIU investigator, clinical reviewer, document analyst, and customer-service writer at the same time. ClaimIQ assigns each responsibility to a focused stage with clear authority limits.

Business transformation

The platform targets the end-to-end insurance claims value chain: intake, document validation, coverage reasoning, fraud scoring, clinical triage, and adjuster decision support.

  • Reduces manual reading and re-keying from every incoming claim.
  • Standardises extraction and review criteria across adjusters.
  • Turns incomplete submissions into instant structured customer requests.

Agentic workflow

Each agent receives upstream outputs, adds its own specialist view, writes an auditable result, and passes structured context forward. The pipeline remains dependency-aware instead of becoming a loose chatbot chain.

  • Mail Guard prevents non-claims from consuming downstream capacity.
  • Intake creates the canonical claim record.
  • Copilot synthesises, but does not approve or deny.

Resilience by design

Every agent has a deterministic fallback. If an LLM call fails, the workflow continues with tagged fallback output so reviewers can see what happened and why.

  • Immutable fraud score before LLM explanation.
  • Emergency clinical hard overrides.
  • Coverage compliance checks on AI and fallback outputs.

Operating model

From raw email to adjuster-ready brief.

01

Receive and validate

ClaimIQ reads the inbound email via Gmail IMAP. Input validation guards against oversized or malformed payloads, and Mail Guard checks for claimant name, policy number, incident date, and incident description. A confirmation email is sent immediately. General enquiries trigger a rewrite-request email with the submission form — no downstream agents run.

02

Extract and route

Intake extracts 30+ structured fields and classifies attachments using vision and text models, uploading them to a per-claim Google Drive folder. The router then selects and orders exactly the downstream agents this claim needs, enforcing dependencies and pausing for missing documents.

03

Reason with evidence

Coverage cites policy sections before any position is surfaced. Fraud computes an immutable, claim-type-weighted SIU score before the LLM explains it. Triage applies clinical urgency rules with hard overrides — emergency terms cannot be downgraded by any downstream output. Mid-pipeline status emails keep the claimant informed.

04

Synthesise and deliver

The Copilot prepares a multi-role decision brief with citations, draft letters, and next steps. A PDF adjuster guide and a conditional detailed report are generated and emailed with a Google Drive link. The adjuster completes the decision via a pre-filled Google Form linked to the claim ID in BigQuery and Looker Studio.

Project overview

ClaimIQ is an AI-powered, multi-agent insurance claims platform built for Track 1 (industry and business transformation) of the HCLTech–OpenAI Agentic AI Hackathon. It transforms a raw claim email and its attachments into a structured claim record, a policy-aware coverage view, a fraud risk profile, a clinical triage decision, and an adjuster-ready decision brief — while keeping a human in control of every consequential decision.

Problem being solved

  • Manual claim review can take 45 to 90 minutes, and complex health claims can take multiple hours.
  • Unstructured emails and documents create inconsistent extraction, missed details, and slow customer follow-up.
  • Policy wording is dense, making coverage decisions risky without cited evidence.
  • Fraud review is often reactive instead of proactive and signal-driven.
  • Clinical urgency is difficult for non-clinical claims teams to prioritise safely.

ClaimIQ outcome

  • Structured claim facts extracted from email and attachments in 5-7 minutes.
  • Coverage status with cited policy sections and a regulatory compliance checklist.
  • Deterministic fraud score, triggered SIU signals, and a recommended investigation action.
  • Clinical priority, colour, routing queue, SLA, and specialist recommendation.
  • Copilot brief tailored for the claims officer, medical reviewer, fraud investigator, supervisor, audit, and customer-service teams.

Glossary — click a term to expand

Agentic workflow

A pipeline where specialised AI agents each own one bounded responsibility, exchange strict JSON contracts, and are orchestrated in dependency order — as opposed to a single monolithic prompt. ClaimIQ runs seven such agents per claim.

ClaimSession

The immutable, versioned state object created per claim. Agents read snapshots and append outputs; nothing mutates shared state, which makes every run replayable and auditable.

Deterministic fallback

A rule-based path (regex extraction, weighted scoring, hard triage rules) that produces a safe result whenever an OpenAI call fails or is disabled. Every ClaimIQ agent has one, and fallback reasons are tagged in the output.

SIU signal

A Special Investigation Unit fraud indicator — one of 13 deterministic checks (duplicate claim, identity mismatch, billing anomaly, …) each carrying a claim-type-specific weight that sums into the 0–100 fraud score.

UM reviewer

Utilisation-management reviewer — the clinical role the Triage agent emulates when assessing medical urgency, mapping emergency terms and vitals to priority, queue, SLA, and specialist.

Human approval gate

A mandatory stop before any consequential outcome. Triggered by a high fraud score, coverage uncertainty, high claim value, sensitive claim types, or duplicates — the AI prepares evidence, a human decides.

Explainability JSON

A delimited JSON block (CLAIMIQ_EXPLAINABILITY_JSON_BEGIN … END) emitted at the end of every run, containing per-agent outputs, timings, and reasons. The Streamlit console parses it to render the per-claim explainability view.

Rewrite request

Mail Guard's structured reply when a claim email is missing required fields — it lists exactly what is missing and includes a resend form, halting the pipeline until the customer resubmits.

Agent responsibilities

Each agent owns its own folder with three files — agent.py (the public run() entry point, audit events, and fallback selection), tool.py (OpenAI prompt construction and external lookups), and functions.py (deterministic business logic used for fallback and guardrails). This keeps every agent independently testable and its behaviour predictable even when the LLM is unavailable.

00 Mail Guard gpt-4o-mini · front-door gate

Classifies each inbound email as a usable claim or a rewrite request before any downstream agent runs. It requires four fields — claimant_name, policy_number, incident_date, and incident_description — and rejects general enquiries, placeholder or fabricated policy numbers, future-dated incidents, and vague intent. Below a 0.50 confidence threshold it returns a structured rewrite request with a resend form. Model: gpt-4o-mini, with a keyword-based deterministic fallback.

In: subject + body + dateOut: proceed / rewrite_requestFallback: keyword + regex guard
01 Intake Agent o4-mini + gpt-4o-mini vision · 30+ field contract

Extracts 30+ structured fields (claimant, patient, policy, claim type, incident date, diagnosis, procedure, hospital, amount, currency, vehicle, location, risk indicators, missing information, and a narrative summary). It classifies attachments into document types — health card, prescription, MRI report, hospital estimate, pre-auth form, KYC, FIR/police report, fire-brigade NOC, damage photo, repair invoice, property policy — checks required documents per claim type, flags quality issues (blurry, cropped, unreadable) and red-flag terms (edited, mismatch, tamper, forged, duplicate), and sets an intake status of complete, needs_review, or incomplete. Reasoning: o4-mini; vision: gpt-4o-mini; deterministic regex fallback.

In: email + attachmentsOut: structured claim + document summaryFallback: regex extraction
02 Router gpt-5.5 (ambiguous only) · agent selection

Picks and orders only the downstream agents a claim needs. A deterministic fast path routes all four downstream agents when intake is complete or needs_review (no LLM call); an OpenAI-backed router (gpt-5.5) handles ambiguous states; and incomplete intake pauses the pipeline for a customer document request. Dependencies are always enforced — copilot requires triage, and triage requires coverage and fraud.

In: intake status + session snapshotOut: selected_agents in orderFallback: heuristic route
03 Coverage Agent o4-mini · policy evidence + compliance

Looks up the policy in BigQuery, searches per-type policy PDFs (health, motor, property, travel), and reasons about active status, covered perils, waiting-period breach, exclusions, sub-limits, and remaining balance. It returns a coverage status of covered, needs_review, or not_covered, then applies a regulatory-compliance layer that blocks any denial lacking a cited exclusion or clause and always attaches an appeals/grievance process. Model: o4-mini, with deterministic coverage from the policy record as fallback.

In: claim facts + policy recordOut: coverage status + citationsFallback: policy-record rules
04 Fraud Agent o4-mini (explain only) · immutable score

Computes 13 deterministic SIU signals with claim-type-specific weights, sums them into a 0–100 score, and assigns a risk band before any LLM synthesis. The LLM (o4-mini) can only explain the fixed score — it cannot revise it or convert evidence into an accusation. See the signal matrix and the interactive simulator below.

In: evidence + claim historyOut: score 0–100 + signals + actionFallback: deterministic brief
05 Triage Agent o4-mini over hard rules · queue + SLA

Acts like a clinical nurse plus a utilisation-management reviewer. It detects emergency, urgent, elective, and anomaly signals — including non-medical urgency for motor, property, and travel (total loss, displacement, evacuation, repatriation) — then assigns colour, priority, routing queue, SLA, specialist, and human-review requirement. Hard overrides ensure emergencies can never be downgraded. Model: o4-mini over deterministic hard rules.

In: coverage + fraud + clinical factsOut: queue, colour, SLA, specialistFallback: hard-rule triage
06 Adjuster Copilot o4-mini · multi-role brief v2.0

Synthesises all upstream outputs into a versioned (brief v2.0) multi-role decision brief: executive summary, claim details, coverage position, fraud assessment, routing decision, open questions, human-review checklist, evidence log, role-specific assistance, draft customer/adjuster letters, and a Markdown brief. It assists but never approves, denies, settles, or accuses. Model: o4-mini, with a structured fallback brief assembled deterministically.

In: all agent outputsOut: decision brief + draft lettersFallback: structured brief assembly

    Pipeline contract

    A session-based orchestrator owns an immutable ClaimSession with a versioned workflow state, and runs the stages in dependency order: Mail Guard, Intake, Router, Coverage, Fraud, Triage, and Copilot. Each stage returns structured JSON and writes start/completion events with durations so later agents can build on earlier decisions. All events are designed for BigQuery audit tables when persistence is enabled. At the end of every run the pipeline emits a delimited explainability JSON block (CLAIMIQ_EXPLAINABILITY_JSON_BEGIN … END) that the Streamlit console parses and renders per claim.

    Interactive — pipeline stage explorer

    Click any stage to inspect its model, contract, customer email, fallback, and audit events. Use ←/→ arrow keys to step through the pipeline.

    Workflow state machine

    The claim session advances through explicit states, each recorded on the immutable session so the workflow is fully replayable:

    • claim_receivedEmail accepted; instant acknowledgment sent to the claimant.
    • guard_passedMail Guard validated the four required fields (or issued a rewrite request).
    • intake_completeStructured claim record created from body + attachments.
    • routing_decidedRouter selected and ordered the downstream agents (or paused for documents).
    • agents_runningCoverage, Fraud, Triage, and Copilot execute with per-stage timing.
    • complete / partial / failedTerminal state; report generated and assessment email sent.

    • claim_receivedInstant acknowledgment when the email arrives and passes Mail Guard.
    • coverage_verified / coverage_needs_reviewSent after the Coverage agent, depending on outcome.

    • Looker Studio: a live dashboard reads the BigQuery claim tables for queue, risk, and SLA monitoring.

    Governance and safety guardrails

    Human authority

    ClaimIQ prepares evidence and recommendations. It does not make final approval, rejection, settlement, denial, or accusation decisions.

    Coverage compliance

    Coverage cannot deny without cited policy evidence. Missing critical fields force review, and appeals guidance is included in the coverage output.

    Fraud integrity

    Fraud score is computed by deterministic signals first. The LLM can explain the score but cannot rewrite the score or turn investigation evidence into an accusation.

    Clinical safety

    Emergency terms such as cardiac arrest, stroke, respiratory failure, sepsis, or major trauma trigger hard overrides so urgent claims cannot be downgraded.

    Auditability

    Agent starts, completions, durations, statuses, and full outputs — plus a versioned workflow state on every claim session — are designed for BigQuery audit tables when persistence is enabled.

    Input validation

    Oversized or malformed submissions are treated as a customer-communication problem, not a crash. They route to the rewrite path with a structured resend form instead of failing the poll cycle.

    Resilience

    Every major step has a deterministic fallback path and tags fallback reasons, making failure modes visible instead of silent. The whole pipeline can run offline with CLAIMIQ_USE_OPENAI=false.

    Input validation & injection guards

    Externally supplied fields are bounded before they reach any agent. Limits are environment-overridable so operations can raise them without a code change, and a failed check routes to the customer rewrite path rather than crashing the poll cycle.

    FieldLimitEnv override
    Email body50,000 charsCLAIMIQ_MAX_EMAIL_LENGTH
    Subject500 charsCLAIMIQ_MAX_SUBJECT_LENGTH
    Policy number50 chars + India/UK/US formatCLAIMIQ_MAX_POLICY_NUMBER_LENGTH
    Claim amount₹50,000,000 (5 crore)CLAIMIQ_MAX_CLAIM_AMOUNT_INR
    Uploaded documents25 filesCLAIMIQ_MAX_UPLOADED_DOCUMENTS

    A forbidden-pattern scan rejects injection payloads such as <script>, eval(, os.system(, and subprocess.run( in the email body and subject.

    Responsible AI summary

    ClaimIQ is decision support, not autonomous settlement authority. High-risk and high-value claims trigger mandatory human approval; coverage uncertainty routes to human review; fraud signals are evidence-linked and explainable but never accusatory; agent failures fall back to safe deterministic outputs; and customer-facing emails describe assessment status and next steps rather than final legal liability.

    Python pipeline (local run)

    1. Clone the ClaimIQ hackathon repo from Nikhil0075/ClaimIQ_AI_Hackathon ↗ and cd into the project root.
    2. Install dependencies: pip install -r requirements.txt (or pip install -e .).
    3. Copy .env.example to .env and set OPENAI_API_KEY, Gmail credentials, and Google OAuth credentials path.
    4. Deterministic smoke test (no external services): python -m claimiq.pipeline.orchestrator --sample tests/sample_claim.json --pretty
    5. Run the built-in demo (no Gmail needed): python app/run.py --demo
    6. Watch Gmail continuously: python app/run.py --watch
    7. Operator console: streamlit run app/streamlit_app.py
    8. Skip BigQuery writes: add --no-bq to any command. Run tests with pytest -q.

    1. Gmail: enable two-step verification, create an app password, and set GMAIL_ADDRESS + GMAIL_APP_PASSWORD. IMAP is used for reads, SMTP over SSL for sends.
    2. Drive: create an OAuth 2.0 client ID, download the JSON, set GOOGLE_OAUTH_CREDENTIALS, and run once to complete the consent flow (token caches to ~/.claimiq/drive_token.json).

    4. Looker Studio: point a report at the claim tables and set LOOKER_URL for the console link.
